## 4. Key Insights

- Credit history is one of the strongest indicators of loan approval and repayment reliability.
- Applicants with high loan amounts compared with income may represent higher repayment risk.
- Income should be analyzed together with loan amount to understand affordability.
- Education, property area, gender, and dependents can help identify approval patterns across customer groups.
- Risk is highest when multiple weak indicators appear together, such as low income, no credit history, and high loan amount.

## 5. Business Recommendations

- Use a risk-based approval process instead of relying on one factor.
- Review applicants with weak credit history using additional verification checks.
- Calculate debt-to-income ratio before approving large loans.
- Offer adjusted loan terms, co-applicant options, or lower loan amounts for higher-risk customers.
- Monitor approved high-risk customers after disbursement to catch repayment issues early.
